Accessibility Crash Course

The legal landscape

In the United States, baseline expectations for web usability are written into law in The Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and Section 504 and 508 of the Rehabilitation Act.

Because your thesis or dissertation will be published in the University's Digital Conservancy, it must meet the University's Accessibility of Digital Content and Information Technology policy, which adheres to federal law.

Optional: If you are a fan of documentation and technical specifications, the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) lay out a series of conditions for making web content more accessible to a wider range of people with disabilities.

References

  • Rehabilitation Act of 1973 prohibits discrimination on the basis of disability for the government or anyone receiving federal funding.
    • Section 504 helps ensure that students with disabilities have equal access to educational opportunities.
    • Section 508 addresses electronic and information technology
  • Americans with Disabilities Act approaches accessibility requirements through 4 different sectors.
    • Title I is related to employment.
    • Title II addresses public services, which includes publicly funded institutions.
