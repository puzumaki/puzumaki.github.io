Course structure overview

This course is broken up into modules. All modules can be found by clicking on the Modules option in the course's navigation menu. The activities you encounter throughout are for you, we will not request a copy to complete the course.

The course content will begin after this introduction.

Outline of modules

  1. About the course (you are here)
    • Provides an overview of the course content and how to navigate.
    • Summary: 10 minutes
  2. Introduction to disability and accessibility
    • Become familiar with basic terms and concepts used to describe disability and accessibility.
    • Be introduced to the idea of digital accessibility and how it can be incorporated into your daily work.
    • Recognize the changing legal landscape around accessibility as one reason why accessibility matters.
    • Summary: 40 minutes with 4 optional readings
  3. Core skills in digital accessibility
    • Learn how you can build content to be more accessible from the start.
    • Be introduced to core (universal) digital accessibility skills.
    • Understand how these changes improve experience through demos and activities.
    • Summary: 45 to 60 minutes with 7 activities
  4. Checking and saving digital documents
    • Learn how to use accessibility checkers.
    • Understand the limitations of the PDF format.
    • How to make a PDF accessible.
    • Summary: 30 minutes
  5. Course review and completion
    • Verify you have completed all the content.
    • Complete the course.

Take time to reflect and prepare yourself to include accessibility in the way you think. Select "Next" on the bottom right of the screen to continue on to the next page. If you get lost, go back to Modules for the course outline and find your place again.
