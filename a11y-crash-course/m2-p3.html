Disability is a spectrum

Disabilities can be apparent to you — or not. They can be permanent, temporary, or situational.

For instance, someone born d/Deaf is considered by society to have a permanent disability. However, someone with an ear infection that temporarily affects their hearing could be described as having a temporary disability. A bartender who is struggling to hear while in a noisy workspace could be described as having a situational disability.

The Persona Spectrum from Microsoft's Inclusive 101 Guidebook.

Considering the wide range of disabilities in the world can help to design materials differently, extending them to more people in new ways.

Note on language: "d/Deaf" is used here to support both deaf as a medical status and cultural status. The National Center on Disability and Journalism's (NCDJ) Disability Language Style Guide explains: "Many people in the Deaf community prefer the use of a lowercase “d” to refer to audiological status and the use of a capital “D” when referring to the culture and community of Deaf people."
