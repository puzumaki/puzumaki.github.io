Accessibility is everyone's job

In the social model of disability, society can amplify exclusion and discrimination against people with disabilities, but it also can drive people to design a world that works well for a wider range of bodies and minds. When understood in this light, every person is responsible for contributing to the accessibility of their environment.

Key point: Accessibility is never “someone else’s job.” You may have a variety of roles at a school or workplace, and all of those roles can increase or decrease access for people with disabilities.
