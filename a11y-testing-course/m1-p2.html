Why digital accessibility testing

We are required to make sure all our digital assets are accessible to all participants. Here are a few links for reference:

  • Rehabilitation Act of 1973 prohibits discrimination on the basis of disability for the government or anyone receiving federal funding.
    • Section 504 helps ensure that students with disabilities have equal access to educational opportunities.
    • Section 508 addresses electronic and information technology
  • Americans with Disabilities Act approaches accessibility requirements through 4 different sectors.
    • Title I is related to employment.
    • Title II addresses public services, which includes publicly funded institutions.

This course is developed with the intention of building skills and capacity for all of us to share in the responsibility of ensuring equal access. The course focuses on commitment to the work and less so the compliance aspect.

What disabilities are impacted?

  • Visual: blindness, low vision, color blindness
  • Auditory: deafness, hard of hearing
  • Motor: paralysis, cerebral palsy, missing or damaged limbs
  • Cognitive: learning disabilities, traumatic brain injury, Dyslexia
  • Seizure: epilepsy
  • Mental health: depression, anxiety, panic attacks
