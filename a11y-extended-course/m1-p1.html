Module overview: Accessibility and accommodation

Welcome to Module 1: Accessibility and accommodation. Did you know that, according to the National Center for Education Statistics (NCES), in 2015 almost 20% of undergraduate students and 12% of graduate students identify as disabled? University staff and faculty also may identify as disabled, with 27% of the general U.S. population recognized by the government as disabled. In this module, you will:

  • articulate the differences between accommodations and accessibility,
  • become familiar with accommodations processes on your campus and recognize student perspectives on accommodations, and
  • locate appropriate accommodations and accessibility resources on your campus.

Key points

  • The best way we can support people is by making content, services, and spaces as accessible as possible to minimize the need to request accommodations.
  • Accessibility is proactive while accommodation is reactive.
  • Universities have disability services offices for supporting accommodations.

Thank you for continuing to make your library a more accessible place.
