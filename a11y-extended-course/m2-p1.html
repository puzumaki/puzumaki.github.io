Module overview: Implicit biases and disability

Welcome to Module 2: Implicit biases and disability. It will introduce the concept of implicit bias and how it produces microaggressions that can impact classroom, work, and social environments. In this module, you will:

  • define implicit bias and microaggressions, and understand how the two concepts are related,
  • become familiar with tools and strategies to interrupt bias and microaggressions, and
  • identify resources for communication strategies and places for further personal work.

Key points

  • The actions and decisions resulting from implicit biases can create real world barriers to equity and opportunity.
  • Implicit bias is important to understand, particularly those biases that don't align with intentions, or explicit beliefs.
  • Interrupt bias by pausing, asking, listening, and speaking your truth or sharing stories.
