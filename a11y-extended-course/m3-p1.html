Module overview: Practical strategies for effective communication and interaction

The resources provide a starting point for how to effectively communicate with people with disabilities. The point is to ensure respectful communication between all human beings, make our interactions more effective, and make people more comfortable.

Disability etiquette uses common sense and courtesy. Treat other people the way you would want to be treated. In this module, you will:

  • learn how to show respect through action and words,
  • go through examples for access in interactions, and
  • take action through effective apologies.

Throughout this module are sections from a piece titled “Be an ally to disabled people”. They offer ways to ally with disabled people, the words coming from disability communities.

Key points

  • Respect people through language and action, follow their cues.
  • Educate yourself by centering voices of the disability community.
  • Confront ableism and genuinely apologize if harm is done.

Be an ally to disabled people

Disabled people are everywhere, and yet are often invisible to others. Everyone grew up learning stereotypes about disabled people. Some of these stereotypes are negative (“helpless” or “dependent”). Others seem positive but are just as limiting (“inspiring” or “brave”). In order to end disability oppression (called ableism), nondisabled people need to learn how to be allies to disabled people.
