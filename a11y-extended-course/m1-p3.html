Become familiar with the accommodation process

Students with disabilities at your university likely go through an individualized process facilitated by their student disability center. The process requires paperwork and medical documentation, and may require students to resubmit paperwork every semester.

Employees with disclosed disabilities go through a similar process via Human Resources or their employee disability center, though usually with less-frequent paperwork submission than for students. University guests may sometimes also go through an abbreviated process with the campus' disability center.

In this 1 minute and 27 second video, the Disability Resource Center at the University of Minnesota discusses what such an individualized process may look like (or skip to the transcript).

Key point: The best we can support people is by making content, services, and spaces as accessible as possible to minimize the need to request accommodations.

Video transcript

Disability services works in partnership with University of Minnesota students, faculty, staff, and guests, to advance equal opportunities for learning, working, and participating in campus life. Our role is to facilitate access; we level the playing field for people with disabilities by addressing barriers in the physical, program, information, and attitudinal environments.

Disabilities services provides services to individuals with a variety of disability conditions. The majority of individuals registered have invisible or non-apparent disabilities, such as mental health conditions, learning disabilities, and chronic health issues.

We use an interactive and collaborative process for the determination of reasonable accommodations, where each partner [student or employee, disability services provider, and faculty or manager] in the process has an integral role.

  • The student or employee brings their knowledge about the barriers they encounter in the learning or working environment.
  • Faculty or managers bring their knowledge about the essential elements of the course or essential duties of the job.
  • And the disability services specialists bring their knowledge about potential accommodations, alterations, or modifications that may remove or reduce the identified barriers.

Through this process, barriers are identified, and reasonable accommodations that do not compromise the essential elements of the course or job are determined and implemented.
